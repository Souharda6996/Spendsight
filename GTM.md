# GTM.md — SpendSight Go-to-Market Strategy

## Target User (Specific)

**CTO or Engineering Lead at a 5–30 person Series A startup** spending $500–5,000/month on AI tools who has never done a structured audit.

Characteristics:
- Subscribed to 3+ AI tools organically (one team member tried Cursor, another uses Claude, the API team runs GPT-4 directly)
- Spends appear on the company card as separate line items; no one has totaled them
- Would feel mildly embarrassed if a board member asked "what's your AI spend?" — they don't know
- Time-poor; will not fill out a 20-field form or book a demo to see results

**They are NOT:** an individual developer shopping for personal tools. The value prop is about *team* spend and *organizational* oversight.

---

## What They Search For

Based on realistic search intent, not keyword volume:

- `claude vs chatgpt for developers` — comparison shopping, usually before a purchase decision
- `reduce AI api costs 2025` — already feeling the pain, actively seeking solutions
- `github copilot worth it for small team` — questioning current spend, open to alternatives
- `cursor pro vs github copilot team` — actively evaluating tools for a team
- `anthropic api pricing calculator` — technical buyers doing math before committing
- `AI tools for startups budget` — founders thinking about tool spend holistically

## Where They Are Online

| Channel | Specific Location | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Reddit | r/ExperiencedDevs, r/startups | Senior devs and founders; high signal:noise; genuine peer discussion |
| Slack | Indie Hackers #tools, On Deck Founders, Ramen Club | Decision-makers, already discussing tool stacks |
| X (Twitter) | Dev-founders who post about AI tooling | Viral coefficient high; CTOs with 1k–50k followers share useful tools |
| Hacker News | Show HN on Tuesday 9am ET | Best time slot; YC-adjacent founders are the exact ICP |
| LinkedIn | Eng manager / CTO posts about AI spend | Lower engagement than X but higher B2B intent |

## First 100 Users — Tactical Playbook

**Day 1 (while deploying):**
1. Post in 5 Slack communities: *"Built a free tool to audit AI spend for dev teams — took 2 days, would love brutal feedback. No signup needed: [link]"* — the "roast it" framing gets more clicks than "check out my product"

**Day 2:**
2. Cold DM 20 CTOs on X who've publicly complained about AI bills in the last 30 days. Message: *"Saw your tweet about [AI tool] costs. Built a free audit tool this week — takes 60 seconds, curious what it finds for you."*

**Day 3:**
3. Show HN: *"Show HN: SpendSight – Free AI spend audit for startup teams (no signup)"* — Post at Tuesday 9am ET. Keep the comment short and factual.

**Day 4:**
4. One authentic founder LinkedIn post: *"Audited our AI tools last night. Found $340/mo waste in 5 minutes. Built this to help other teams do the same: [link]. The biggest surprise: we were paying for GitHub Copilot Business at 1 seat."*

**Day 5:**
5. Post in r/ExperiencedDevs with genuine context: *"We audited our AI subscriptions and found we were paying GitHub Copilot Business rates for a single developer ($19 vs $10). Built a free tool that catches these. Would love feedback from people who've done this manually."*

## Unfair Channel

**Credex's existing customer list.** Email the audit tool as a free value-add before any pitch. Customers already trust Credex for credit sourcing. An unsolicited useful tool converts to consultations at 10–20% vs cold outreach at 1–2%. This is the week-1 lever that no competitor can copy.

---

## Week 1 Success Metrics (If GTM Is Working)

| Metric | Target | Leading Indicator |
|---|---|---|
| Audits completed | 500 | 100 by end of Day 2 |
| Email capture rate | 16%+ (80 emails) | Any rate >10% means results are landing |
| Consultation bookings | 5 | Requires ≥1 person with >$500 savings |
| Shared audit links created | 40+ | If sharing < 5%, results page isn't good enough |

If audits are completing but email capture rate is <10% after 200 audits, the issue is the results page — either the savings are too low or the email CTA placement is wrong.
